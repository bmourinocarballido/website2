Beatriz Mouriño Carballido
I am an Associate Professor at the University of Vigo and a member of the Biological Oceanography Group. I lead a multidisciplinary team studying how the ocean works and why it matters, focusing on the two-way interactions between physics and biology—how turbulence and nutrient supply shape microscopic algae, and how biological processes like bioturbulence influence ocean dynamics. These tiny organisms produce as much oxygen as all the world’s forests, absorb carbon dioxide, and help slow climate change. We explore these connections using satellite images, fieldwork, lab experiments, and computer models. If you want to know more about my CV, click here.
